# VAST Challenge 2026 Submission Instructions

## Deadline

The submission deadline is **July 17, 2026 at 11:59 pm AOE**.

Participants are permitted to submit multiple entries, but each entry must contain the complete set of required materials as specified below.

---

## Note on GitHub Entries

In recent years there has been an increase in submissions through GitHub including links to live demonstrations. This is great! However, please submit your entry with a **GitHub tag or as a release**. In addition, entry forms must be submitted in full prior to the deadline. It is not permissible to provide a link to an entry form in GitHub. Entries cannot be updated once submitted unless specifically requested by the VAST Challenge committee.

---

## Required Materials

Your completed submission package is due on **July 17, 2026**. Your submission package consists of a completed summary form and a **four-minute video** showing your visual analytics software in operation.

### Summary Form

Please complete the summary form as follows:

1. **Rename** the summary form file to `index.htm`. Make sure to leave the form in `.htm` format. Use Microsoft Word or other word processor to edit the form.

2. **Name your entry** using a composite of your team's organization, primary contact's last name, and the challenge you are entering.
   - Example: `UMD-Jones-MC2` for a University of Maryland submission for Mini-Challenge 2, led by Dr. Jones.
   - If submitting multiple entries, add a number to the end (e.g., `UMD-Jones-MC2-1`).

3. **Provide a list of team members**, their affiliation, and email addresses. Designate one team member as the **Primary Contact** — this person must be able to respond to questions and communications related to the submission.

4. **Indicate if this is a student team.** A student team is defined as one led by a student and worked on by students. Class projects are good examples.

5. **Provide a list of analytic tools** used in developing your solution.
   - Commercially available tools (e.g., Tableau, SAS) can be mentioned by name.
   - For non-commercial tools, provide a tool name, developer/company name, and links for more information.
   - For tools developed by the submitting team, include additional context (e.g., where and when it was developed, the course, instructor, etc.).

6. **Estimate the number of hours** spent by the entire team on this submission. If submitting multiple entries, this number should reflect time spent on this specific entry.

7. **Indicate whether you give permission** for your submission to be posted in the publicly accessible Visual Analytics Benchmark Repository.

### Video

Provide an explanatory video with voice narration of **no more than four minutes**. The video should be in `.wmv` format.

- If size permits, include the video in your electronic submission package.
- If not, post it online and provide a link. Verify the link remains active throughout the contest period.
- If you do not have a hosting site, contact [vast_challenge@ieeevis.org](mailto:vast_challenge@ieeevis.org) to discuss alternatives.

**Video tips:**

- Clearly explain how your visual analytics software helped you analyze the data.
- Describe your starting point for analysis and how you selected it.
- Explain your rationale for important design decisions.
- Explain how the visualizations and interactions aided the analysis.
- If filters or transformations are applied, explain what was used and in what sequence.
- If anomalies are shown, explain how they factor into the analysis.
- Carefully define any terms used (e.g., "uncertainty").
- Describe any important assumptions you made.
- Answer the specific questions for this mini-challenge (these appear in the submission form).

## Packaging and Submitting Your Entry

1. Rename the summary form to `index.htm` and save it locally. Use this form to provide your answers — either by adding text directly or by linking to separate files.

   > **Note:** GitHub-based entries must also provide a **PDF of their entry form**.

2. Create a folder on your computer named after your entry. Save the following inside:
   - `index.htm` (completed answer form)
   - Design image
   - Optional video and storyboards
   - Other images

   The `index.htm` file should link to all your materials using **relative links** so they still work when moved to another folder.

3. Zip the entire directory using the same entry name as the filename (e.g., `UMD-Jones-MC2.zip`).
   - If your zip file exceeds **50 MB**, post your video or other large materials online instead of including them.
   - The submission system only accepts files **smaller than 50 MB**.

   > **Note:** Entries must be packaged using **ZIP**. Other formats (rar, tar, etc.) are not acceptable.

4. Submit via the **Precision Conference System (PCS)** at [https://new.precisionconference.com/~vgtc/](https://new.precisionconference.com/~vgtc/).
   - Register if you do not already have a login.
   - Once logged in, click **"Submissions"** and select:
     - Society: **VGTC**
     - Conference/Journal: **VIS 2026**
     - Track: **VIS 2026 VAST Challenge**

## Optional Two-Page Summaries

Participants may publish two-page summaries of their submission in the Conference Proceedings. These summaries allow contestants to:

- Give a general overview of their approach and tools
- Highlight novel features
- Provide references to papers and other relevant work
- Describe any new discoveries made while working through the challenge

The two-page summary must be formatted according to the **IEEE VGTC Guidelines** and submitted separately from your entry.

> **Due date: August 14, 2026** *(revised date — required for inclusion in the IEEE conference proceedings)*
